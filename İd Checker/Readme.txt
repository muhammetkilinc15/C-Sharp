*The last 2 letters of the mlik numbers have the confirmatory properties of the first 9 digits.
An expansion between the first 9 digits gives us the 10th digit, if it is common among the first 10 digits, it gives us the 11th digit.
The first digit of ID numbers can never be 0.

** When the sum of the 2nd, 4th, 6th and 8th digits is subtracted from 7 times the sum of the 1st, 3rd, 5th, 7th and 9th digits of our ID numbers,
the remainder of the division by 10, that is, mod 10, gives us the 10th digit. . 1. 2. 3. 4. 5. 6. 7. 8. 
The remainder of the result obtained from the sum of the 9th and 10th digits divided by 10, that is, mod10, gives us the 11th digit.
